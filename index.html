Pan d' Mie

Bread Flour 100% 22 oz
Instant Dry Yeast 0.9% .2 oz
Salt 2.3% .5 oz
Sugar 4.1% .9 oz
Olive Oil 8.8% 1.9 oz
Water 64.9% 14.3 oz
  1. pre-heat oven to 375 deg f.
  2. Combine flour and yeast. Add the ssalt, sugar, oil and water to the mixer and then add the flour and yeast. Mix on low speed with the dough hook attachment for 4 minutes and on medium speed for 4 minutes. The dough should be a little sticky, but with fairly good gluten development. Mix to the intense stage of gluten development.
  3. Bulk ferment the dough until nearly doubled, about 45 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into pieces 2 lb 8 oz each.
  5. Preshape the dough into large oblongs 10 in long Let the dough rest, covered, until relaxed, 15 to 20 minutes. (Reminder: When Making multiple loaves, workd sequentially, starting with the first piece of dough you divided and rounded.)
  6. Shape each loaf into a 15" oblong and place in your pans. Cover with a damp towel and let rise 45 to 60 mintues or until the dough is almost to the top of the pan.
  7. Once dough has risen and is slightly springy to the touch, place pan lid firmly on and place in the oven. Bake for 40 minuts/until the internal temperature of the bread is 270 deg f.
  8. Imediately remove from the pan onto a cooling rack. Let bread completely cool before slicing.

Challah

Bread Flour 100% 22 oz
Instant Dry Yeast 0.9% .2 oz
Salt 2.3% .5 oz
Sugar 4.1% .9 oz
Olive Oil 8.8% 1.9 oz
Water 64.9% 14.3 oz
Eggs 1.4% 5.55 oz
  1. Combine flour and yeast. Add the salt, sugar, oil and water to the mixer. Beat the eggs, reserve 1 tbsp. and add rest to the oil mixture. Add the flour and yeast. Mix on low speed with the dough hook attachment for 4 minutes and on medium speed for 4 minutes. The dough should be a little sticky, but with fairly good gluten development. Mix to the intense stage of gluten development.
  2. Remove the dough from the bowl and oil it with 1 tbsp oil. Return the dough and bulk ferment the dough until nearly doubled, about 45 minutes.
  3. Divide the dough into pieces 11 oz each.
  4. Preshape the dough into balls and let the dough rest, covered, until relaxed, 15 to 20 minutes. (Reminder: When Making multiple loaves, workd sequentially, starting with the first piece of dough you divided and rounded.)

Apple Pie

Jonny Crisp Apples/pealed and sliced-thick 6 lbs
Apply Juice 8 floz
Corn Starch 2 oz
Granulated White Sugar 1 lb
Salt 1 oz
Cinnamon/ground 1 oz
All Spice/ground .5 oz
Nutmeg/ground .25 oz
Vanilla Extract 2 floz
Pie Dough/See Recipe 2 ea
  1. In an 8 qt pot, combine all ingredients, reserving the vanilla extract. cover and cook on medium heat until apples are semi-soft, approximately 40 minutes.
  2. Remove the apples from the heat and chill in the refrigerator until cool.
  3. Roll out your pie dough into two 12 inch disks for your top and bottom crust. line your pie pan with one disk.
  4. Remove the chilled apple mixture from the refrigerator and mix in the vanilla extract.
  5. Pre-heat the oven to 375 deg F.
  6. Fill the lined pan with the apple mixture and top with the second pie douch disk. Crimp the edges of the two disks together to seal in the apple mixture. Create vent slits in the top of the pie to allow steam to escape while baking.
  7. Brush the top of the pie with egg wash and bake in oven for 40-45 minutes.
  8. 5 minutes before pie is completed baking you can lightly spray with water and dust with large granulated sugar for additional effect.
  9. Allow to cool completely before serving.
